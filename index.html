The book

An annual reference, built to be kept

Irish whiskey has grown from a handful of working distilleries to more than forty in little over a decade, and it has no annual record. Scotch has had one since 2005. The wine world has had one far longer. BONDED is the first reference written to mark each year in Irish whiskey as it happens.

It serves two readers at once. The visitor who has just finished a distillery tour and wants to understand what they tasted, and the enthusiast who already knows their pot still from their malt and grain. Each section goes deeper than the last, so both find their way in.

The object

A5 hardback, 256 pages. Section-sewn and cloth-bound in bog oak with copper foil. Midnight blue endpapers, a silk ribbon marker, printed on FSC certified stock. Sized to carry to a distillery or a tasting, which is the whole promise of a handbook.

The first edition is limited to 2,000 hand-numbered copies.

The inaugural focus

The year of Single Pot Still

The first edition takes single pot still as its subject. The style that is Ireland's own, and the one now at the heart of a proposed amendment to the Irish Whiskey Technical File.

A style returned to the centre of the conversation.
